1. Sai Baba Temple Shirdi

- Located at a distance of 258 km from Mumbai rests the very famous Shirdi Sai Baba Temple. The temple is known for being the abode of Sai. Every year, the town of Shirdi witnesses a massive footfall of followers and devotees who come seeking blessings of Sai Baba. Sai Baba is known to have been one of the greatest saints. The town of Shirdi is where Sai Baba attained Samadhi and hence, is a popular temple in Maharashtra. Over the years, the temple town has also been transformed into a very modern place with many tourist attractions.

2. Ganpatipule Temple

- Located on the coast of Konkan in Maharashtra lies the beautiful holy town of Ganpatipule. The small pilgrimage town is about 345 km from Mumbai and is close to the town of Ratnagiri. One of the main reasons it is named Ganpatipule is because it is famous for its 400-year-old Ganpati Temple. The alluring temple is located right on the beach and is believed for the self-incarnated idol of Ganesha. Ganpatipule is definitely one of the popular temples to visit in Maharashtra. Not only can you make a good weekend trip to visit the temple but also stay there with your family to soak in the serene atmosphere.

3. Trimbakeshwar Temple

- Stationed in the lovely locales of Trimbak in Maharashtra is the popular Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga temple. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and has been attracting a plethora of pilgrims for a long time now. It is extremely popular for its Kushavarta Kund in Trimbak, which is a sacred bathing venue. The kund receives its water from the Godavari River. The temple was constructed by Peshwa Balaji Baji Rao and has three lingams of Brahma, Vishnu and Shiva in the premise. It is known to be one of the most famous and best temples in Maharashtra..

4. Bhimashankar Temple

- Not only is Bhimashankar one of the most famous treks in Maharashtra, but it is also a popular temple in the state. Burrowed in the luscious green Sahyadri hills is the popular temple in Maharashtra. Bhimashankar Temple is undoubtedly one of the famous temples to visit in Maharashtra that should be on your list of religious places. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the temple is considered to be one of the 12 Jyotirlinga shrines in the country. Out of the 12 Jyotirlingas in the country, five of them are located in Maharashtra alone.
