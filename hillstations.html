-Hill Stations in Maharastra-


1. Lonavala

- Lonavala is a popular hill station in Maharashtra located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra..

- It is one of the best hill station in Maharashtra and attracts tourists from all over India.

- Lonavala is famous for its scenic beauty, lush greenery, waterfalls, and caves.

- Lonavala is also a popular destination for adventure activities like trekking, camping, and paragliding.

- The highest point in Lonavala is the Duke's Nose, which offers a panoramic view of the surrounding hills and valleys.

2. Khandala

- Khandala is one of the most popular hill stations in Maharashtra, situated in the Western Ghats.

- It is just a few kilometers away from Lonavala, another famous hill station in Maharashtra.

- Khandala is surrounded by lush green valleys, cascading waterfalls, and misty mountains, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers.

- The hill station is known for its scenic views of the Western Ghats, which are visible from several viewpoints in Khandala.

3. Matheran

- Matheran is situated in the Raigad district of Maharashtra and is about 80 km from Mumbai.

- It is famous for its natural beauty and is a popular weekend getaway for people living in nearby cities like Mumbai and Pune.

- Matheran is one of the smallest hill stations in India and is also known for being Asia's only automobile-free hill station.

- The town is located at an elevation of 2,625 feet above sea level, making it the highest hill station in the Raigad district.

4. Mahabaleshwar

- Mahabaleshwar is situated in the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra, at an altitude of 1,353 meters above sea level.

- Mahabaleshwar is famous for its scenic beauty, lush green forests, and numerous waterfalls.

- It is often referred to as the 'Queen of Hill Stations' in Maharashtra.

- It is also known for its strawberry farms, which produce some of the best strawberries in the country.

5. Igatpuri

- Located in the Western Ghats, Igatpuri is surrounded by lush green forests and misty hills, offering a serene escape from the chaos of city life.

- Igatpuri is also home to the Vipassana International Academy, a meditation center that attracts people from all over the world.

- The Bhatsa River Valley and the Camel Valley are two popular tourist spots in Igatpuri that offer stunning views of nature.

- The hill station is well-connected by road and rail, making it easily accessible from Mumbai and other cities in Maharashtra.
